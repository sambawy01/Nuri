# 1. MATHEMATICS (Cambridge Primary Mathematics 0845/0096)

The Cambridge Primary Mathematics curriculum is presented in five content areas (strands): **Number**, **Geometry**, **Measure**, **Handling Data** (Statistics), and **Problem Solving**. Each strand is further divided into sub-strands. Problem solving underpins all other strands.

## Coding System (0845 version)

Each learning objective has a unique code, e.g. **1Nn1** where:
- First digit = Stage number (1-6)
- Letters = Sub-strand code
- Final number = Objective number within that sub-strand

### Sub-strand Codes:
- **Nn** — Numbers and the number system
- **Nc** — Calculation (mental strategies, addition/subtraction, multiplication/division)
- **Nf** — Fractions (in some versions)
- **Gs** — Geometry: Shapes
- **Gp** — Geometry: Position and movement
- **Ml** — Measure: Length, mass, capacity
- **Mm** — Measure: Money
- **Mt** — Measure: Time
- **Ma** — Measure: Area and perimeter (Stages 4-6)
- **Dh** — Handling data
- **Db** — Probability (Stages 5-6)
- **Pt** — Problem solving: Using techniques and skills
- **Ps** — Problem solving: Using understanding and strategies

## STAGE 1 (Year 1)

### Number — Numbers and the Number System (Nn)
- **1Nn1–1Nn12:** Recite numbers in order (at least to 100); read and write numerals 0-20; count objects reliably; understand counting conservation; count on in twos and tens; begin to count on in fives; even numbers as "every other number"; partition two-digit numbers into tens and ones; compare two-digit numbers using > and <; order numbers to at least 20; understand and use ordinal numbers; use the = sign to represent equality; estimate a quantity by giving a range of numbers

### Number — Calculation (Nc)
**Mental strategies (1Nc1–1Nc7):** Know all number pairs to 10 and pairs with a total of 6, 7, 8, 9, 10; add more than two small numbers; begin bridging through 10; know doubles to at least double 5; identify near doubles; begin to know multiplication facts for 2× and 10× tables

**Addition and Subtraction (1Nc8–1Nc18):** Understand addition as combining groups and counting on; understand subtraction as counting back, taking away, and finding a difference; record addition and subtraction sentences; know that addition can be done in any order (commutativity); add/subtract single-digit numbers to/from two-digit numbers

**Multiplication and Division (1Nc19–1Nc22):** Double single-digit numbers; halve even numbers to 10; share objects into equal groups; find simple remainders after division

### Geometry — Shapes (Gs)
- **1Gs1–1Gs3:** Name and sort common 2D shapes (circles, squares, rectangles, triangles); identify 3D shapes (cubes, cuboids, cylinders, spheres, cones, pyramids) and describe properties; recognise basic line symmetry

### Geometry — Position (Gp)
- **1Gp1:** Use language of direction and distance (left, right, up, down, forwards, backwards)

### Measure — Money (Mm)
- **1Mm1:** Recognise all coins and notes; use money to pay for items using exact amounts

### Measure — Length, Mass, Capacity (Ml)
- **1Ml1–1Ml3:** Compare lengths, masses, and capacities by direct comparison; estimate and measure using non-standard units; use comparative language (longer, shorter, heavier, lighter, more, less)

### Measure — Time (Mt)
- **1Mt1–1Mt3:** Know units of time (seconds, minutes, hours, days, weeks, months, years); read analogue clocks to the hour (o'clock); order the days of the week

### Handling Data (Dh)
- **1Dh1:** Answer a question by sorting and organising data in block graphs, pictograms, lists, tables, Venn and Carroll diagrams

### Problem Solving (Pt, Ps)
- **1Pt1–1Pt9:** Choose appropriate mental strategy for calculations; explain methods and reasoning; explore number puzzles; find possible combinations; create and solve word problems; check answers; describe and continue patterns; estimate and check

## STAGE 2 (Year 2)

### Number — Numbers and the Number System (Nn)
- **2Nn1–2Nn19:** Read and write numbers to at least 100; count objects (groups of tens and ones); count on/back in ones, tens, twos, and fives; partition two-digit numbers; find 1 or 10 more/less than a number; round two-digit numbers to nearest 10; use ordinal numbers; compare and order numbers to 100; estimate quantities; recognise odd and even numbers; understand fraction notation (halves and quarters); find halves and quarters of shapes and small numbers; recognise equivalence between 1/2 and 2/4

### Number — Calculation (Nc)
**Mental strategies (2Nc1–2Nc5):** Know pairs that total 10 and 20; partition to 20; pairs of multiples of 10 totalling 100; begin to know multiplication facts for 2×, 5×, 10× tables; know doubles to at least 20

**Addition/Subtraction (2Nc6–2Nc15):** Count on/back in tens from two-digit numbers; represent equality; add 4 or 5 small numbers; find unknown in number sentences (□ + 3 = 10); add/subtract single-digit to/from two-digit numbers; add pairs of two-digit numbers; find differences by counting up

**Multiplication/Division (2Nc16–2Nc23):** Understand multiplication as repeated addition and arrays; understand division as grouping and sharing; double multiples of 5 to 50; double two-digit numbers; begin to learn 3× and 4× tables; understand remainders in division

### Geometry — Shapes (Gs)
- **2Gs1–2Gs4:** Sort, name, describe, and draw 2D shapes (squares, rectangles, circles, triangles, pentagons, hexagons); identify properties of 3D shapes; find reflective symmetry in patterns and shapes; identify shapes in the environment

### Geometry — Position (Gp)
- **2Gp1–2Gp3:** Follow and give instructions for position, direction, and movement; understand whole, half, and quarter turns; recognise right angles in turns

### Measure — Money (Mm)
- **2Mm1–2Mm3:** Recognise all coins and notes; use money notation; find totals and give change

### Measure — Length, Mass, Capacity (Ml)
- **2Ml1–2Ml2:** Estimate, measure, and compare lengths, masses, and capacities using standard units (cm, m, 100g, kg, litre)

### Measure — Time (Mt)
- **2Mt1–2Mt5:** Know time units and relationships between them; read clocks to the half hour; measure activities in seconds and minutes; order months of the year and days of the week

### Handling Data (Dh)
- **2Dh1–2Dh2:** Collect and organise data in lists, tables, block graphs, pictograms, Carroll and Venn diagrams; use one and two criteria for sorting

### Problem Solving (Pt, Ps)
- **2Pt1–2Pt11:** Choose mental strategies; explain methods; investigate puzzles; solve one-step and two-step word problems; create number stories; check calculations; describe patterns and relationships; estimate and check reasonableness

## STAGE 3 (Year 3)

### Number — Numbers and the Number System (Nn)
- **3Nn1–3Nn20:** Recite numbers 100–200 and beyond; read and write numbers to 1000; count in ones, tens, hundreds; count in steps of 2, 3, 4, and 5; understand place value in three-digit numbers; find 1, 10, or 100 more/less; multiply single-digit numbers by 10; round two-digit numbers to nearest 10 and three-digit numbers to nearest 100; place numbers on a number line; compare and order three-digit numbers; estimate by approximation; understand halves of odd and even numbers; use fraction notation (3/4 as "three quarters"); recognise equivalences (1/2 = 2/4 = 4/8 = 5/10); understand mixed numbers; order unit fractions; understand fraction as division; find halves, thirds, quarters, and tenths of shapes and numbers

### Number — Calculation (Nc)
**Mental strategies (3Nc1–3Nc8):** Know addition/subtraction facts to 20; add/subtract multiples of 100 to 1000; know 2×, 3×, 5×, 10× tables; begin 4× table; recognise multiples of 2, 5, 10; know doubles to 20 and doubles of multiples of 10

**Addition/Subtraction (3Nc9–3Nc18):** Add/subtract 10 and multiples of 10; add 100 to three-digit numbers; add several small numbers; find complements to 100; add/subtract pairs of two-digit numbers; add three-digit and two-digit numbers; reorder additions; add/subtract near multiples of 10; find 20/30...100/200/300 more or less

**Multiplication/Division (3Nc19–3Nc26):** Understand relationship between halving and doubling; multiply two-digit numbers by 10; multiply/divide by 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, 10; understand remainders; know commutativity of multiplication; understand multiplication/division relationship

### Geometry — Shapes (Gs)
- **3Gs1–3Gs8:** Identify and describe regular/irregular 2D shapes including pentagons, hexagons, octagons, semi-circles; classify by properties (sides, vertices, right angles); identify 3D shapes including pyramids and prisms; explore cube nets; identify 2D shapes with reflective symmetry; create reflections using mirror lines; relate 2D/3D shapes to drawings; identify right angles in 2D shapes

### Geometry — Position (Gp)
- **3Gp1–3Gp4:** Use direction/movement language (clockwise, anti-clockwise); read/use grid positions with labelled rows/columns; draw and compare angles to right angles; understand a straight line = two right angles

### Measure — Money (Mm)
- **3Mm1–3Mm2:** Consolidate money notation; calculate change using complements to 100

### Measure — Length, Mass, Capacity (Ml)
- **3Ml1–3Ml5:** Choose appropriate units and equipment; know relationships (km/m, m/cm, kg/g, l/ml); read scales to nearest division; measure with ruler to nearest cm; solve word problems involving measures

### Measure — Time (Mt)
- **3Mt1–3Mt4:** Choose time units and know relationships; read analogue clocks to nearest 5 minutes and digital clocks to nearest minute; calculate simple time intervals; use calendars and calculate week/day intervals

### Handling Data (Dh)
- **3Dh1–3Dh3:** Investigate real-life questions with data collection, organisation, and interpretation; use tally charts, frequency tables, pictograms (1-2 unit symbols), bar charts; use Venn/Carroll diagrams with two criteria

### Problem Solving (Pt, Ps)
- **3Pt1–3Pt12:** Choose mental strategies; understand measurement systems; solve one-step and two-step word problems (all four operations); check calculations; recognise 2D/3D relationships; estimate and approximate
- **3Ps1–3Ps9:** Create number stories; explain strategies; explore puzzles and logic problems; use ordered lists/tables; describe patterns (steps of 2, 3, 4, 5, 10, 100); identify number and shape relationships; investigate general statements; explain reasoning orally

## STAGE 4 (Year 4)

### Number — Numbers and the Number System (Nn)
- **4Nn1–4Nn25:** Read/write numbers to 10,000; count in various steps including thousands; use decimal notation for tenths and hundredths (money and length contexts); find multiples of 10/100/1000 more/less; multiply/divide by 10 and begin by 100; recognise multiples; round 3-4 digit numbers; use empty number lines; estimate; understand negative numbers in context; recognise number sequences; explore odd/even properties; make general statements about odd/even sums/differences; order fractions with same denominator; understand fraction equivalences (1/2 = 4/8 = 5/10; 1/4 = 2/8; 1/5 = 2/10); use equivalences for ordering; understand decimal/fraction equivalence (0.5 = 1/2 = 5/10); recognise mixed numbers (5 3/4); understand fraction/division relationship; find halves, quarters, thirds, fifths, eighths, tenths of shapes and numbers

### Number — Calculation (Nc)
**Mental strategies (4Nc1–4Nc16):** Know pairs of two-digit numbers totalling 100; pairs of multiples of 50 totalling 1000; simple fraction pairs totalling 1; know all tables to 10×10; add 3-4 small numbers; add three two-digit multiples of 10; add/subtract near multiples of 10/100; add/subtract pairs of two-digit numbers; find differences between near multiples of 100; multiply single-digit pairs; use commutativity; understand effect of multiplying by 10; know doubles to 50, 500, 5000 and corresponding halves

**Addition/Subtraction (4Nc17–4Nc19):** Add pairs of three-digit numbers; subtract two-digit from three-digit; subtract pairs of three-digit numbers

**Multiplication/Division (4Nc20–4Nc26):** Double two-digit numbers; multiply multiples of 10 (10-90); multiply two-digit by single-digit; divide two-digit by single-digit (answers ≤20); understand rounding after division; use multiplication/division as inverses; introduce simple ratio and proportion

### Geometry — Shapes (Gs)
- **4Gs1–4Gs5:** Identify wider range of 2D/3D shapes (quadrilaterals, heptagons, tetrahedra); use pinboards and spotty paper; classify polygons (right angles, regularity, symmetry); identify lines of reflective symmetry; visualise 3D from 2D nets/drawings; identify common solid nets; find shapes and symmetry in the environment and art

### Geometry — Position (Gp)
- **4Gp1–4Gp3:** Read and describe grid positions (numbered/lettered rows/columns); measure angles in degrees (360° = full turn, 4 right angles = 360°); compare and order angles less than 180°; describe paths and directions

### Measure — Length, Mass, Capacity (Ml)
- **4Ml1–4Ml4:** Use standard metric units with abbreviations (km, m, cm, mm, kg, g, l, ml); know relationships between familiar units; understand kilo, centi, milli prefixes; use decimal notation for measures (1.3 m, 0.6 kg, 1.2 l); interpret partially numbered scales

### Measure — Time (Mt)
- **4Mt1–4Mt4:** Read time to nearest minute (12-hour digital and analogue); use a.m./p.m. notation; read simple timetables; use calendars; choose appropriate time units

### Measure — Area and Perimeter (Ma)
- **4Ma1–4Ma3:** Measure and calculate rectangle perimeters; understand area measured in square units (cm²); find areas of rectilinear shapes by counting squares

### Handling Data (Dh)
- **4Dh1–4Dh3:** Identify data to collect; organise and present data in tables, diagrams, tally charts, frequency tables, pictograms (2/5/10/20 unit symbols), bar charts; compare impact of different scale intervals; use Venn/Carroll diagrams with two-three criteria

### Problem Solving (Pt, Ps)
- **4Pt1–4Pt8:** Choose mental/written strategies; understand measurement systems; check addition (by reordering/subtraction), subtraction (by addition), multiplication (different technique), division (by multiplication); recognise 2D/3D relationships; estimate and approximate
- **4Ps1–4Ps9:** Create number stories (measures context); explain reasoning for multiplication/division; solve addition/subtraction problems with explanation; explore puzzles/logic; use ordered lists/tables; describe and continue sequences; identify shape relationships; investigate general statements; test hypotheses

## STAGE 5 (Year 5)

### Number — Numbers and the Number System (Nn)
- **5Nn1–5Nn22:** Count forwards and backwards through zero; understand place value in 5-6 digit numbers; partition numbers to one million; use decimal notation for tenths/hundredths with place value understanding; multiply/divide numbers 1-10,000 by 10 and 100; round 4-digit numbers to nearest 10/100/1000; round one-two decimal place numbers; order/compare numbers to one million; order positive and negative numbers; understand temperature rise/fall; order/compare numbers with one-two decimal places; recognise/extend number sequences; understand odd/even and multiples (5/10/25/50/100) to 1000; make general statements about odd/even sums/differences/multiples; understand fraction equivalences (1/2, 1/4, 1/8; 1/3, 1/6; 1/5, 1/10); compare decimals and fractions; convert improper fractions to mixed numbers (7/4 = 1 3/4); order mixed numbers; understand fraction/division relationship; find fractions of quantities; understand percentages as parts of 100; calculate simple percentages; express halves/tenths/hundredths as percentages; use fractions for proportion; use ratio for problem solving (e.g. recipe adaptation)

### Number — Calculation (Nc)
**Mental strategies (5Nc1–5Nc17):** Know pairs of one-place decimals totalling 1 and 10; know all tables to 10×10; test divisibility by 2, 5, 10, 100; know multiples of 6-9; know square numbers to 10×10; identify two-digit factors; count on/back through thousands/hundreds/tens/ones; add/subtract near multiples of 10/100; strategies for two-three digit numbers with one decimal place; find differences near 1000 and near 1; multiply multiples of 10-90 and 100-900; multiply by 19/21 (via 20); multiply by 25 (via 100÷4); use factor multiplication; double to 100 and 200; double/halve one-two decimal numbers; double/halve multiples of 10-1000

**Addition/Subtraction (5Nc18–5Nc19):** Find totals of 3+ two-three digit numbers; add/subtract three-four digit numbers with same decimal places (including money)

**Multiplication/Division (5Nc20–5Nc27):** Multiply/divide three-digit by single-digit; multiply two-digit by two-digit; multiply one decimal place numbers by two-digit; divide three-digit by single-digit with remainders (answers ≤30); express remainder as fraction of divisor; decide when to group/share in division; round division answers context-dependently; use brackets for operation ordering; understand four operations relationships and arithmetic laws

### Geometry — Shapes (Gs)
- **5Gs1–5Gs7:** Classify triangles (isosceles, equilateral, scalene); identify regular polygon symmetry (reflective and rotational); create patterns with two lines of symmetry; visualise 3D shapes from 2D nets (cube variants); identify perpendicular and parallel lines; measure angles in degrees (nearest 5°); classify angles as acute, right, or obtuse; know angles on a straight line

### Geometry — Position (Gp)
- **5Gp1–5Gp3:** Read and plot co-ordinates in first quadrant; predict reflections (mirror parallel to side, including oblique lines); understand and predict translations; give translation instructions

### Measure — Length, Mass, Capacity (Ml)
- **5Ml1–5Ml7:** Read and select standard units with suitable accuracy; convert larger to smaller metric units (using decimals to one place); order mixed units; round to nearest whole unit; interpret unnumbered scale divisions; compare readings from different scales; measure/draw lines in cm and mm

### Measure — Time (Mt)
- **5Mt1–5Mt6:** Know time units (seconds through years); compare 24-hour digital and analogue clocks; read 24-hour timetables; calculate time intervals (seconds/minutes/hours); calculate calendar day/week intervals; calculate month/year intervals

### Measure — Area and Perimeter (Ma)
- **5Ma1–5Ma3:** Measure and calculate perimeters of regular/irregular polygons; understand area in square centimetres; calculate rectangle areas using formula (length × width)

### Handling Data (Dh)
- **5Dh1–5Dh5:** Develop question sets with data collection/selection/organisation; draw conclusions from data; identify further questions; interpret frequency tables, pictograms, bar line charts (various vertical axis scales); understand vertical axis scale impact; interpret simple line graphs (e.g. temperature over time); understand intermediate point meaning; identify and interpret the mode

### Probability (Db)
- **5Db1:** Use language of chance and likelihood to describe familiar events

### Problem Solving (Pt, Ps)
- **5Pt1–5Pt7:** Understand measurement systems for calculations; solve single/multi-step word problems (four operations); check using reordering/inverse; recognise 2D/3D relationships; estimate/approximate/round; check reasonableness
- **5Ps1–5Ps10:** Use measurement-based calculations; select strategies with explanation; explore puzzles/logic; deduce from existing information; use ordered lists/tables; describe sequences (e.g. –30, –27, –24, –21, –18); identify shape relationships; investigate general statements; test hypotheses with explanation; break problems into sub-problems

## STAGE 6 (Year 6)

### Number — Numbers and the Number System (Nn)
- **6Nn1–6Nn30:** Count in fractions, decimals, and whole numbers (including through zero); understand place value to one million; represent one-two decimal places; multiply/divide 1-10,000 by 10/100/1000; multiply/divide decimals by 10/100; find two-digit factors; find common multiples; round whole numbers to 10/100/1000; round two decimal places to nearest tenth or whole; estimate and justify large numbers; order/compare to one million and negative integers; use >, <, = correctly; estimate on 0-10,000 number lines; order numbers with up to two decimal places (varying places); recognise/extend sequences; understand three decimal place decimals in measurement contexts; identify odd/even, multiples, and prime numbers to 20 and <100; explore number system origins and development; compare fractions using denominators (3/4 with 7/8); understand equivalences (1/100s, 1/10s, 1/2s); convert improper fractions to mixed numbers (17/8 = 2 1/8); reduce fractions to simplest form; convert vulgar fractions to decimals via division; express percentages (1/2, 1/4, 1/3, 1/10, 1/100); find simple percentages of shapes and whole numbers; solve simple ratio and proportion problems

### Number — Calculation (Nc)
**Mental strategies (6Nc1–6Nc10):** Know addition/subtraction facts within 20; one-place decimal pairs totalling 1; one-place decimal pairs totalling 10; two-place decimal pairs totalling 1; test divisibility (2/4/5/10/25/100); add/subtract two-digit wholes and three-digit multiples of 10; add/subtract decimal pairs (e.g. 2.6+2.7, 0.78+0.23); adjust near multiples of 10/100/1000; use place value and multiplication facts for mental division/multiplication (0.8×7, 4.8÷6); multiply pairs of multiples of 10 (30×40, 600×40); double two-digit numbers (78, 7.8, 0.78) and find halves; divide two-digit by single-digit mentally

**Addition/Subtraction (6Nc11–6Nc13):** Add/subtract two-three digit numbers with same/different decimal places including money; find differences between positive and negative integers; find differences between two negative integers (temperature/number line contexts)

**Multiplication/Division (6Nc14–6Nc22):** Multiply multiples of 10 pairs (30×40); multiply 10/100 multiples (600×40); adjust near 10 multiples; use halving-doubling (35×16 as 70×8); generate new facts from known facts (17× from 10×+7×); multiply two-four digit by single-digit (including money); multiply two-three digit by two-digit; divide three-digit by single-digit (with remainder); divide three-digit by two-digit (no remainder, including money); express division answers as mixed numbers and decimals; use fraction operators (tenths/hundredths of quantities); apply commutativity, associativity, distributivity

### Geometry — Shapes (Gs)
- **6Gs1–6Gs6:** Classify polygons and identify non-polygons; describe 3D shape properties (faces, edges, vertices); classify quadrilaterals (parallelogram, rhombus, trapezium) by properties (parallel sides, equal sides/angles); create 2D representations of 3D shapes including nets; estimate and measure angles with protractor (nearest degree); understand triangle angle sum (180°); calculate angles in triangles and at points

### Geometry — Position (Gp)
- **6Gp1–6Gp2:** Read and plot co-ordinates in all four quadrants; predict reflections (including non-parallel/perpendicular sides to mirror); predict translations; predict 90° rotations about a vertex

### Measure — Length, Mass, Capacity (Ml)
- **6Ml1–6Ml5:** Select standard units with two-three decimal place reading; convert units (kg/g, l/ml, km/m/cm/mm) using three decimal places (1.245 m = 1 m 24.5 cm); interpret scales from range of instruments; measure/draw lines in cm/mm; know imperial units still in use (mile) and metric approximations

### Measure — Time (Mt)
- **6Mt1–6Mt8:** Understand time units (seconds through centuries) with conversions; use 24-hour digital and analogue clocks; compare 24-hour formats; read/use 24-hour timetables; calculate 24-hour time intervals; calculate calendar day/week/month intervals; calculate month/year intervals; understand time zone differences worldwide

### Measure — Area and Perimeter (Ma)
- **6Ma1–6Ma3:** Measure/calculate perimeters and areas of rectilinear shapes; estimate irregular shape areas (square counting); calculate compound shape perimeters/areas (rectangular split)

### Handling Data (Dh)
- **6Dh1–6Dh4:** Represent/extract/interpret data from tables, graphs, charts, diagrams including line graphs (distance-time), price ready-reckoners, frequency tables, grouped discrete bar charts; identify mode and range; begin to understand median and mean; explore everyday statistics

### Probability (Db)
- **6Db1:** Use probability language; assess likelihood and risk; identify events with equally likely outcomes

### Problem Solving (Pt, Ps)
- **6Pt1–6Pt5:** Select appropriate/efficient mental/written strategies; understand measurement systems; check addition by reordering and subtraction by inverse; recognise 2D/3D shapes and relationships; estimate/approximate/round for checking
- **6Ps1–6Ps9:** Explain strategy choices with working; deduce from information with effect recognition; use logical reasoning for puzzles; use systematic solving with ordered lists/tables; generalise number relationships using words, symbols, letters (n, 2n+5, 3n-1, triangle angle sum 180°); solve single/multi-step word problems with diagrams and brackets; solve ratio/proportion word problems; solve percentage word problems (discounts); make/test/refine hypotheses with explanation

# 2. SCIENCE (Cambridge Primary Science 0846/0097)

**0846 version strands:** Scientific Enquiry, Biology, Chemistry, Physics

**0846 version codes:** e.g. 1Ep1, 3Bp2, 4Cs4
- First digit = Stage number
- Letter(s) = Strand and sub-strand
- Final number = Objective number

- First digit = Stage number
- Letters = Strand.sub-strand
- Number after dot = Objective number

## STAGE 1 (Year 1)

### Scientific Enquiry
- **1Ep1:** Explore and observe to find answers to questions
- **1Ep2:** Make predictions about what will happen
- **1Eo1:** Observe and compare objects, living things and events
- **1Eo2:** Record observations using drawings and simple tables

### Biology — Structure and Function (Bs)

# 3. ENGLISH (Cambridge Primary English 0844/0058)

The Cambridge Primary English curriculum is organised into three main strands: **Reading**, **Writing**, and **Speaking and Listening**.

## Coding System

**0844 version codes:** e.g. 5Rw3, 3Wp1
- First digit = Stage number
- Letter(s) = Strand and sub-strand
- Final number = Objective number

**0058 version codes:** e.g. 1Rw.01, 3Wg.05
- First digit = Stage number
- Letters = Strand.sub-strand
- Number after dot = Objective number

# 4. HISTORY (Primary Level)

## Important Note

Cambridge Assessment International Education (CAIE) does **not** offer a specific standalone "History" curriculum for the primary level. History at Cambridge Primary is addressed through:

1. **Cambridge Primary Global Perspectives (0838)** — which incorporates historical thinking within cross-curricular challenges
2. **School-level curricula** — most British/Cambridge international schools use the **English National Curriculum for History** (KS1 and KS2) for their history teaching

The content below is based on the **English National Curriculum History Programmes of Study** (published September 2013), which is the framework most commonly used by Cambridge-affiliated and British international schools worldwide.

**Source:** [National Curriculum in England: History Programmes of Study (GOV.UK)](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-curriculum-in-england-history-programmes-of-study/national-curriculum-in-england-history-programmes-of-study)

---

## Purpose of Study

A high-quality history education helps pupils gain a coherent knowledge and understanding of Britain's past and that of the wider world. It inspires pupils' curiosity to know more about the past, equips them to ask perceptive questions, think critically, weigh evidence, sift arguments, and develop perspective and judgement.

## Curriculum Aims

All pupils should:
- Know and understand the history of Britain as a coherent, chronological narrative, from the earliest times to the present day
- Know and understand significant aspects of the history of the wider world: ancient civilisations, empires, and non-European societies
- Gain and deploy a historically grounded understanding of abstract terms such as "empire", "civilisation", "parliament", and "peasantry"
- Understand historical concepts such as continuity and change, cause and consequence, similarity, difference, and significance
- Understand the methods of historical enquiry, how evidence is used rigorously to make historical claims, and understand how and why contrasting arguments and interpretations of the past have been constructed
- Gain historical perspective by placing their growing knowledge into different contexts

---

## KEY STAGE 1: Years 1-2

### Required Subject Content

Pupils should develop an awareness of the past, using common words and phrases relating to the passing of time. They should know where people and events they study fit within a chronological framework and identify similarities and differences between ways of life in different periods.

#### 1. Changes Within Living Memory
- Focus on aspects of change in national life (where appropriate, revealed through the lives of significant individuals)
- **Commonly taught topics:** History of toys (39.9% of schools), seaside holidays (24.8%), transport (19.3%)

#### 2. Events Beyond Living Memory (nationally or globally significant)
- Events that are significant nationally or globally
- **Commonly taught topics:** The Great Fire of London (72% of schools), the first aeroplane flight, space exploration/Moon landing (16%), the Gunpowder Plot (15%)

#### 3. Lives of Significant Individuals
- People who have contributed to national and international achievements
- Some should be used to compare aspects of life in different periods
- **Commonly taught individuals:** Florence Nightingale (26.8%), Mary Seacole (22.7%), Neil Armstrong, Christopher Columbus, Rosa Parks, Samuel Pepys, Emmeline Pankhurst

#### 4. Significant Historical Events, People and Places in Their Own Locality
- Local history study connecting to broader historical themes

### Historical Skills (KS1)
- Place events and objects in chronological order
- Use common words and phrases about the passing of time (old, new, a long time ago, before I was born)
- Identify similarities and differences between ways of life in different periods
- Use a wide vocabulary of everyday historical terms
- Ask and answer questions, choosing and using parts of stories and other sources to show understanding
- Understand some of the ways in which we find out about the past
- Identify different ways in which the past is represented

## KEY STAGE 2: Years 3-6

### Required Subject Content

Pupils should continue to develop a chronologically secure knowledge and understanding of British, local, and world history, establishing clear narratives within and across the periods they study. They should note connections, contrasts, and trends over time and develop the appropriate use of historical terms.

#### British History

**1. Changes in Britain from the Stone Age to the Iron Age**
- Late Neolithic hunter-gatherers and early farmers (Skara Brae)
- Bronze Age religion, technology, and travel (Stonehenge)
- Iron Age hill forts: tribal kingdoms, farming, art and culture
- **Typically taught:** Year 3

**2. The Roman Empire and Its Impact on Britain**
- Julius Caesar's attempted invasion in 55-54 BC
- The Roman Empire by AD 42 and the power of its army
- Successful invasion by Claudius and conquest, including Hadrian's Wall
- British resistance, e.g. Boudica
- "Romanisation" of Britain: sites such as Caerwent and the impact of technology, culture, and beliefs, including early Christianity
- **Typically taught:** Year 3 or 4

**3. Britain's Settlement by Anglo-Saxons and Scots**
- Roman withdrawal from Britain in c. AD 410 and the fall of the western Roman Empire
- Scots invasions from Ireland to north Britain (now Scotland)
- Anglo-Saxon invasions, settlements, and kingdoms: place names and village life
- Anglo-Saxon art and culture
- Christian conversion — Canterbury, Iona, and Lindisfarne
- **Typically taught:** Year 4 or 5

**4. The Viking and Anglo-Saxon Struggle for the Kingdom of England to the Time of Edward the Confessor**
- Viking raids and invasion
- Resistance by Alfred the Great and Athelstan, first king of England
- Further Viking invasions and Danegeld
- Anglo-Saxon laws and justice
- Edward the Confessor and his death in 1066
- **Typically taught:** Year 4 or 5

**5. A Local History Study**
- A depth study linked to one of the British areas of study
- A study over time tracing how several aspects of national history are reflected in the locality
- A study of an aspect of history or site dating from a period beyond 1066 that is significant in the locality

**6. A Study of an Aspect or Theme in British History That Extends Pupils' Chronological Knowledge Beyond 1066**
- Schools choose from themes such as:
  - The changing power of monarchs (John, Anne, Victoria)
  - Changes in an aspect of social history (crime and punishment, leisure)
  - The legacy of Greek or Roman culture on later periods
  - A significant turning point in British history (first railways, Battle of Britain)
- **Most commonly taught:** World War Two (55.7%), Victorians (32.2%), Tudors (27.8%)

#### World History

**7. The Achievements of the Earliest Civilisations**
- Overview of where and when the first civilisations appeared
- Depth study of ONE of: Ancient Sumer, The Indus Valley, Ancient Egypt, The Shang Dynasty of Ancient China
- **Most commonly taught:** Ancient Egypt (77.4%)

**8. Ancient Greece**
- A study of Greek life and achievements and their influence on the western world
- **Coverage:** 82.9% of schools (near-universal)

**9. A Non-European Society That Provides Contrasts with British History**
- ONE study chosen from: Early Islamic civilisation (Baghdad c. AD 900), Mayan civilisation (c. AD 900), Benin (West Africa, c. AD 900-1300)
- **Most commonly taught:** Mayans (44.6%), Benin (16.8%), Early Islamic civilisation (12.7%)

### Historical Skills (KS2)
- Establish clear narratives within and across periods studied
- Note connections, contrasts, and trends over time
- Develop appropriate use of historical terms
- Address and devise historically valid questions about change, cause, similarity, difference, and significance
- Construct informed responses involving thoughtful selection and organisation of relevant historical information
- Understand how knowledge of the past is constructed from a range of sources
- Develop increasingly sophisticated understanding of chronology

While the National Curriculum does not prescribe specific topics to specific years, the most common distribution used by British international schools is:

| Year | Typical Topics |
|------|---------------|
| Year 1 | Changes within living memory (toys, transport); Significant individuals (Florence Nightingale, Mary Seacole) |
| Year 2 | Events beyond living memory (Great Fire of London, Moon landing); Significant rulers; Local history |
| Year 3 | Stone Age to Iron Age; Ancient Egypt (earliest civilisations) |
| Year 4 | Roman Empire and its impact on Britain; Anglo-Saxons and Scots |
| Year 5 | Vikings and Anglo-Saxon struggle; Ancient Greece |
| Year 6 | Non-European society (Mayans/Benin/Islamic civilisation); British history beyond 1066 (e.g. WW2, Victorians, Tudors); Local history study |

**Note:** Schools have significant flexibility in arranging these topics. The table above represents the most common arrangement but is not mandated. Some schools teach Ancient Greece in Year 4 and Romans in Year 5, for example.

# APPENDIX: Key Differences Between Curriculum Versions

## Mathematics: 0845 vs 0096
- 0845 uses codes like 1Nn1, 2Gc3 (stage + sub-strand letters + number)
- 0096 uses revised strands and updated learning objectives
- Core content areas remain: Number, Geometry, Measure, Statistics, with Problem Solving integrated throughout

## Science: 0846 vs 0097
- 0846 has four strands: Scientific Enquiry, Biology, Chemistry, Physics
- 0097 has six strands: Thinking and Working Scientifically, Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Earth and Space, Science in Context
- 0097 adds Earth and Space as a standalone strand (previously embedded in other strands)
- 0097 adds Science in Context strand to connect science to learners' lives
- Objective codes change format: 0846 uses "1Bp1" style, 0097 uses "1Bp.01" style

## English: 0844 vs 0058
- 0844 uses codes like 5Rw3 (stage + sub-strand letters + number)
- 0058 uses codes like 5Rw.03 (stage + sub-strand letters + dot + number)
- 0058 reorganises sub-strands for greater clarity (e.g. separating Interpretation from Appreciation in Reading)
- Both maintain the three main strands: Reading, Writing, Speaking and Listening
