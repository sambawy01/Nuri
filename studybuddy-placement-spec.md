# StudyBuddy — Placement Assessment (Registration)

## Why This Matters

A child in Year 6 might read English at a Year 6 level but read Arabic at a Year 2 level. Their school year tells us what curriculum to follow — but their ACTUAL ability determines how Nuri should talk to them, what difficulty to start with, and where the gaps are.

Without this, Nuri might overwhelm a weak Arabic reader with Year 6 grammar, or bore a strong English reader with material that's too easy. The placement test fixes this on day one.

---

## The Experience (How It Feels for the Kid)

This is NOT a scary exam. It's framed as Nuri getting to know them.

> **Nuri:** "Before we start, I want to learn a little about you! I'm going to ask you some quick questions so I know the PERFECT way to help you. It's not a test — there's no right or wrong. Just do your best! Ready? 🦉"

The assessment is:
- **Short** — 5-8 minutes per language (10-16 minutes total)
- **Adaptive** — starts at their year level, goes easier or harder based on answers
- **Fun** — gamified with Nuri reacting, no "you got it wrong" moments
- **Voice-enabled** — they can speak answers (especially important for Arabic)
- **Skippable questions** — "I don't know" is always an option (and it's informative data)

## Assessment Structure

### English Placement (5-8 minutes)

Tests 4 skill areas. Starts at their registered year level, adapts up/down.

#### Area 1: Reading Comprehension (3 questions)
**How it works:**
- Show a short passage (3-6 sentences, age-appropriate)
- Start with a passage at their year level
- Ask 1 comprehension question per passage

**Adaptive logic:**
- Correct → show a passage ONE year higher, ask a harder question
- Wrong → show a passage ONE year lower, ask an easier question
- After 3 questions, the level where they last answered correctly = their reading level

**Question types by level:**
| Level | Passage Complexity | Question Type |
|-------|-------------------|---------------|
| Year 1-2 | 2-3 simple sentences | "What happened?" (literal recall) |
| Year 3-4 | 4-5 sentences with some description | "Why did [character] do that?" (basic inference) |
| Year 5-6 | 6-8 sentences with vocabulary | "What does [word] mean in this sentence?" (inference + vocabulary) |

**Example (Year 3 start):**
> "Tom found a small bird with a broken wing in the garden. He carefully picked it up and brought it inside. His mum helped him make a warm box with soft towels."
> 
> **Nuri asks:** "Why do you think Tom made a box with soft towels?"
> - A) To play with the bird
> - B) To make the bird comfortable while it heals
> - C) Because his mum told him to clean up
> - D) To keep the bird as a pet

#### Area 2: Vocabulary (3 questions)
**How it works:**
- Show a word, ask them to pick the correct meaning or use it in context
- Start at year level, adapt up/down

**Adaptive levels:**
| Level | Example Word | Question |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Year 1-2 | "enormous" | "Which means the same as 'enormous'? A) Tiny B) Very big C) Fast" |
| Year 3-4 | "reluctant" | "She was reluctant to go outside. This means she... A) wanted to B) didn't want to C) forgot to" |
| Year 5-6 | "perseverance" | "Which sentence uses 'perseverance' correctly?" |

#### Area 3: Grammar & Spelling (2 questions)
**How it works:**
- Spot the error or complete the sentence
- Start at year level

**Adaptive levels:**
| Level | Skill Tested | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Year 1-2 | Capital letters, full stops | "Fix this sentence: the dog ran fast" |
| Year 3-4 | Tenses, apostrophes, conjunctions | "Choose the correct word: She ___ (has gone / has went) to the shop" |
| Year 5-6 | Passive voice, relative clauses, semicolons | "Combine these sentences using a relative clause" |

#### Area 4: Writing Expression (1 question — optional)
**How it works:**
- Open prompt: "Tell me about your favourite day ever — in 2-3 sentences"
- Child types or speaks it
- AI evaluates: sentence structure, vocabulary range, grammar accuracy, coherence
- No "right answer" — purely diagnostic

**AI Evaluation Rubric:**
| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| Year 1-2 | Simple sentences, basic vocabulary, some spelling errors, ideas present but basic |
| Year 3-4 | Compound sentences, varied vocabulary, mostly correct grammar, clear sequence |
| Year 5-6 | Complex sentences, rich vocabulary, accurate grammar, descriptive language, cohesive |

### Arabic Placement (5-8 minutes)

This is where placement matters MOST. Many kids in international schools have Arabic that's 2-3 years behind their English. Tests 4 skill areas.

#### Area 1: Letter & Word Recognition (2 questions)
**Starting level based on registered year, adapts down quickly if needed.**

| Level | What's Tested | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| Pre-Year 1 | Letter recognition | "Which letter is this? ب" (show 4 options) |
| Year 1 | Reading 3-letter words | "Read this word: كَتَبَ" (voice input or multiple choice) |
| Year 2 | Reading short sentences | "Read this: الولد يلعب في الحديقة" |
| Year 3-4 | Reading a short passage | "Read this paragraph and tell me what it's about" |
| Year 5-6 | Reading complex text | "Read this passage and answer a question about it" |

**Voice-enabled:** Child can READ ALOUD and Nuri listens (Arabic STT). If they struggle to read, that's placement data.

**"I don't know" option always visible.** If they tap it on letter recognition, Nuri knows to start from the very beginning — no judgment.

#### Area 2: Vocabulary (2 questions)
**How it works:**
- Show an Arabic word, ask for meaning (select from English options)
- Or show an image, ask for the Arabic word

| Level | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Year 1-2 | Show picture of a cat → "What's this in Arabic?" → قطة (options: قطة، كلب، طير) |
| Year 3-4 | "What does مدرسة mean?" → A) Hospital B) School C) House |
| Year 5-6 | "What does اجتهاد mean?" → A) Laziness B) Hard work C) Happiness |

#### Area 3: Grammar (2 questions)
**Only triggered if they pass Area 1 at Year 3+ level. Otherwise skipped.**

| Level | What's Tested | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| Year 3 | Gender (مذكر/مؤنث) | "Which is correct: الولد ذهب / الولد ذهبت?" |
| Year 4 | المبتدأ والخبر | "In this sentence, what is المبتدأ?" |
| Year 5 | إن وأخواتها | "Complete: إنّ العلمَ ___" (نورٌ / نوراً / نورٍ) |
| Year 6 | كان وأخواتها | "Rewrite using كان: الطالب مجتهد" |

**If they can't answer Year 3 grammar → Arabic grammar level set to Year 2 (pre-grammar) regardless of school year.**

#### Area 4: Listening Comprehension (1 question)
**Nuri reads a short passage in Arabic (TTS with Arabic voice). Child answers a question.**

| Level | Passage Length | Question Type |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Year 1-2 | 2 sentences, basic vocabulary | "What was the story about?" (English answer OK) |
| Year 3-4 | 4 sentences | "What happened?" (Arabic or English answer) |
| Year 5-6 | 6 sentences | "Why did [X] happen?" (expects Arabic answer) |

**Critical:** Child can answer in English even for Arabic listening. We're testing comprehension, not production. If they understand Arabic but can't produce it, that's valuable data.

## Adaptive Algorithm

```
For each skill area:
  1. Start at registered_year_level
  2. Ask question at current_level
  3. If correct:
       if current_level < 6:
         current_level += 1
         ask another question
       else:
         skill_level = 6 (max)
  4. If wrong or skipped:
       if current_level > 1:
         current_level -= 1
         ask another question
       else:
         skill_level = 1 (min)
  5. After 3 questions per area (or 2 consecutive wrong):
       skill_level = last level answered correctly
  
  Maximum questions per area: 4 (if bouncing between levels)
  Minimum questions per area: 2 (if clearly at one level)
```

### What Nuri Tells the Child (Post-Assessment)

Nuri NEVER says "your Arabic is weak" or "you're behind." Instead:

> **Nuri:** "All done! Now I know exactly how to help you. 🦉
> 
> Your English is really strong — especially your reading! We'll work on making your vocabulary even bigger.
> 
> For Arabic, I can see you understand a lot when you hear it. We're going to start by building your reading confidence, and you'll be flying through Arabic in no time! 
> 
> Let's get started! ✨"

**The principle:** Always lead with strengths. Frame gaps as opportunities, not deficiencies. Specific about what's good, gentle about what needs work.

## Reassessment

### Automatic Reassessment
- Every 8 weeks, Nuri suggests: "It's been a while since we checked your levels! Want to do a quick check-up? I bet you've improved! 🌟"
- Same adaptive test, fresh questions
- Compare old vs new levels
- If improved: massive celebration. "Your Arabic went from Year 3 to Year 4! That's HUGE! 🎉"
- If same: "You're building a really solid foundation. Keep going!"

### Triggered Reassessment
- If Quiz Mode accuracy in a subject is consistently 90%+ for 2 weeks → suggest upward reassessment
- If Quiz Mode accuracy drops below 40% for a week → suggest checking if the level is too hard

### Manual Reassessment
- Parent or child can request reassessment anytime from Settings

## Registration Flow (Updated)

The full onboarding is now:

```
Step 1: Enter Name
  → "What's your first name?"

Step 2: Select Year Group
  → "What year are you in at school?" (Year 1–6)

Step 3: Pick Avatar Color
  → 6 color options, tap to select

Step 4: English Placement
  → Nuri: "Let's play a quick game so I know how to help you best!"
  → 5-8 minutes, adaptive, fun
  → "Great job! Your English is awesome! ✨"

Step 5: Arabic Placement  
  → Nuri: "Now let's try some Arabic! Remember, it's totally okay to say 'I don't know' 🦉"
  → 5-8 minutes, adaptive, extra gentle
  → "Perfect! Now I know exactly how to help you!"

Step 6: Home Dashboard
  → Profile complete, all subjects visible
  → Nuri: "Welcome to StudyBuddy, {name}! Where do you want to start?"
  → Quick tour of main features (optional, skippable)
```

**Total onboarding: ~15-20 minutes including both assessments.**

**Skip option:** If parent wants to skip assessment (e.g., they know the levels), they can manually set English and Arabic levels in Settings. Nuri gently suggests: "We can always do a quick check-up later to make sure I'm helping you at the right level!"

## Question Bank Requirements

To support adaptive placement, we need a question bank:

**English:** ~60 questions total
- 10 per year level (Year 1–6)
- Mix of reading passages, vocabulary, grammar, spelling
- Multiple variants per level (so reassessment doesn't repeat questions)

**Arabic:** ~60 questions total
- 10 per year level (Year 1–6)  
- Letter recognition, word reading, vocabulary, grammar, listening
- Multiple variants per level
- All Arabic text must include تشكيل (diacritics/vowel marks) for Years 1-3 to aid reading

**Storage:** Pre-built question bank stored in database or JSON. Not AI-generated (needs to be reliable and calibrated). AI is used only for the writing expression evaluation.
